Storyblok wins a lot of teams with its visual editor. Marketers can see the page as they change it, and that is a real draw. But the visual model does not fit every team. Developers sometimes want cleaner typed content, the pricing can climb, and not everyone needs a page builder.

Here are five worth a look. We make one of them, so we have put Inhalt first and made the honest case for it, then laid out the rest.

## Why teams move on from Storyblok

- **Loose, block based content.** Great for pages, harder to reuse cleanly.
- **Pricing that climbs.** Seats and plan limits add up as you grow.
- **A page builder you may not need.** Not every team edits on a canvas.

## What to look for

- **How structured your content is.** Free form blocks, or strict typed fields.
- **How your editors work.** On a visual canvas, or somewhere lighter.
- **Where it runs and what it costs to grow.** Watch the seat and plan limits.

## The best Storyblok alternatives

### 1. Inhalt

**Best for:** teams who would rather say what to change than drag blocks.

Inhalt is ours, so read this as the honest case and weigh the rest freely. It drops the page builder entirely. Your content is exposed through the Model Context Protocol, so an AI client reads it and makes changes by chatting. Instead of moving blocks around, you say what you want changed, and the edit is checked against your schema and written to typed fields. You review the draft and publish when it is ready. It is open source and runs on your own database and keys.

### 2. Contentful

**Best for:** a structured, hosted app without the page builder.

Contentful is the well known hosted option. More structured than Storyblok, less visual.

### 3. Sanity

**Best for:** shaping your own editing experience.

Sanity gives you a flexible model and a studio you can shape, if you want to design the editor yourself.

### 4. Prismic

**Best for:** keeping a visual, slice based flow.

Prismic sits close to Storyblok with its slice based pages, so the jump is small if you like that style.

### 5. Hygraph

**Best for:** typed content feeding many front ends.

Hygraph is built around a typed API, a good fit when the same content reaches several places.

## How to choose

- Would rather just say what to change? **Inhalt**.
- Want more structure in a hosted app? **Contentful**.
- Want to shape the editor yourself? **Sanity**.
- Want a visual, slice based flow? **Prismic**.
- Typed content feeding many front ends? **Hygraph**.
