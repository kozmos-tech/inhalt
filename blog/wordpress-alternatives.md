WordPress runs a huge share of the web, and for a plain site it still does the job. But teams outgrow it. The dashboard gets crowded, plugins pile up, and keeping the whole thing patched turns into a job of its own.

Here are five leaner options. We make one of them, so we have put Inhalt first and made the honest case for it, then laid out the rest.

## Why teams move on from WordPress

- **A crowded dashboard.** More plugins, more menus, more places to get lost.
- **Constant upkeep.** Updates and security patches never really stop.
- **The site is tied to the CMS.** Hard to build a modern front end on top.

## What to look for

- **How content reaches your site.** A theme, or a clean API you can read from any front end.
- **How much you have to maintain.** Plugins and updates add up fast.
- **What editing feels like day to day.** The dashboard is where the time goes.

## The best WordPress alternatives

### 1. Inhalt

**Best for:** teams who are done with the dashboard.

Inhalt is ours, so read this as the honest case and weigh the rest freely. It takes aim at the part people complain about most: there is no admin panel to click through. Your content is exposed through the Model Context Protocol, so the AI client you already use can find an entry and change it by chatting. Every edit is checked against your schema and saved to typed fields, then held as a draft until you publish. It is open source and runs on your own database and keys.

### 2. Ghost

**Best for:** writing heavy sites and newsletters.

Ghost is the clean break for publishing. Fast, focused, and far less to manage than a WordPress install with a dozen plugins.

### 3. A headless CMS (Sanity or Contentful)

**Best for:** a modern front end pulling content through an API.

A headless CMS splits the content from the site, so you build the front end however you want and read content through an API.

### 4. Strapi

**Best for:** self hosting with full control and no seat bill.

Strapi is open source and runs on your own servers, if you want to own the whole stack.

### 5. Webflow

**Best for:** teams who want to design and publish visually.

Webflow suits teams who would rather build and ship pages on a visual canvas than touch code.

## How to choose

- Want the dashboard gone for good? **Inhalt**.
- Mostly publishing articles? **Ghost**.
- Want a modern stack with a separate front end? **A headless CMS**.
- Want to own everything and self host? **Strapi**.
- Prefer to design pages visually? **Webflow**.
