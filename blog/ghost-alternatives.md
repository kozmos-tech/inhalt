Ghost is a lovely tool for writing and newsletters. It is fast, focused, and a relief after a heavy WordPress setup. But it is built around the blog. Once you need more content types, a separate front end, or content that feeds several places at once, you start to feel the edges.

Here are five worth a look. We make one of them, so we have put Inhalt first and made the honest case for it, then laid out the rest.

## Why teams move on from Ghost

- **Built around posts.** Adding other content types gets awkward.
- **The site follows the CMS.** Themes, more than a clean API for any front end.
- **Fine for a blog, tight for an app.** It was made to publish, not to model everything.

## What to look for

- **How many content types you need.** Just posts, or products, docs, and more.
- **Whether the front end is tied to the CMS.** Themes, or a clean API you read from anywhere.
- **How editing feels.** The daily writing and updating flow is what you live with.

## The best Ghost alternatives

### 1. Inhalt

**Best for:** keeping Ghost's light feel, with real typed content and no dashboard.

Inhalt is ours, so read this as the honest case and weigh the rest freely. It keeps the light feeling you liked about Ghost while giving you real typed content. It is exposed through the Model Context Protocol, so the AI client you already use can read your content and change it by chatting. Every edit is checked against your schema and saved to typed fields, then held as a draft until you publish. It is open source and runs on your own database and keys.

### 2. WordPress

**Best for:** flexibility, if you accept the upkeep.

WordPress is the obvious step up in flexibility, though it brings back the plugins and maintenance many people left Ghost to avoid.

### 3. A headless CMS (Sanity)

**Best for:** modeling more than posts and reading through an API.

A headless CMS separates content from the site, so you can model whatever you need and pull it through an API.

### 4. Contentful

**Best for:** a hosted app with structured content types.

Contentful gives you typed content and a hosted editing app, a step up in structure from a blog tool.

### 5. Strapi

**Best for:** self hosting with full control.

Strapi is open source and runs on your own servers when you want to own the whole stack.

## How to choose

- Liked how little Ghost got in your way? **Inhalt**.
- Need lots of flexibility and fine with upkeep? **WordPress**.
- Want a modern stack with a separate front end? **A headless CMS**.
- Want structured content in a hosted app? **Contentful**.
- Want to own the whole stack? **Strapi**.
