Storyblok wins teams with its visual editor. Marketers see the page as they change it, and that is a real draw. Inhalt goes the other way. There is no canvas and no page builder. You say what you want changed, and an AI client makes the edit against typed fields.

Inhalt is ours, so read this as the honest case and weigh it yourself.

## The short version

- **Storyblok** is a hosted CMS built around a visual, block based editor.
- **Inhalt** has no visual editor. Content is exposed through the Model Context Protocol, so an AI client reads it and changes it by chatting.
- Storyblok leans on free form blocks. Inhalt leans on strict typed fields.

## Where they line up

- **Read from anywhere.** Both let any front end pull content through an API.
- **A review step.** Both let you stage a change and publish when it is ready.
- **Content types.** Both let you model more than a plain blog post.

## Where they part ways

### How editors work

Storyblok editors drag and drop blocks on a canvas and watch the page update. Inhalt editors say what they want changed and let the AI client do it. No canvas to learn.

### Structure

Storyblok's blocks are loose and great for one off pages, but harder to reuse cleanly. Inhalt holds content in strict typed fields, so the same content is easy to read anywhere.

### Who runs it and what it costs

Storyblok is hosted and charges by seats and plan limits, which add up as you grow. Inhalt is open source with no seat bill. You run one container on your own database and keys.

## When Storyblok is the better fit

- Your editors want to build and tweak pages on a visual canvas.
- Seeing the page as it changes matters more than strict structure.
- You want a hosted tool with a page builder ready to go.

## When Inhalt is the better fit

- Your team would rather say what to change than drag blocks around.
- You want strict typed content that reads cleanly across many front ends.
- You want no seat bill and your content on your own database.

## The bottom line

If a visual canvas is what your team needs, Storyblok is built for it. If the page builder is not the point for you, Inhalt gives you typed content and a review step, managed from the client you already use. See the [concepts](/docs/concepts) for how it works.
