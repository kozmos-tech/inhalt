Contentful helped make headless content normal: model your types, hand editors a web app, and pull the content into whatever front end you like. It works. But plenty of teams start shopping once the bill climbs, the seat limits bite, or the editing app becomes one more place to sign into.

Here are five worth a real look. We make one of them, so we have put Inhalt first and made the honest case for it, then laid out the rest.

## Why teams move on from Contentful

- **Pricing that jumps.** Seat and record limits push you into the next tier fast.
- **A fixed editing app.** You get Contentful's dashboard, and not much say over it.
- **Vendor lock.** Your content lives in their cloud, not on your own database.

## What to look for

- **Where it runs.** Hosted only, or on your own database and keys.
- **What editing feels like.** A heavy admin app, an inline editor, or something lighter.
- **What it costs to grow.** Watch for per seat pricing and hard caps on types, roles, and calls.

## The best Contentful alternatives

### 1. Inhalt

**Best for:** teams who want the admin app gone entirely.

Inhalt is ours, so read this as the honest case and weigh the rest freely. It takes a different path: there is no dashboard to click through. Your content is exposed through the Model Context Protocol, so the AI client you already use can read your content, find the right entry, and make the change for you. Every edit is checked against your schema and written to real, typed fields. You stage the change, look it over, and publish when it is ready. It is open source and runs on your own database and keys.

### 2. Sanity

**Best for:** teams who want to shape the editing app themselves.

Sanity gives you a flexible content model and a studio you can customize down to the field. That power is real, but the studio is now yours to build and maintain.

### 3. Strapi

**Best for:** teams who want to self host and skip seat pricing.

Strapi is open source and runs on your own servers, with an admin app out of the box. Full control, in exchange for owning the upgrades and hosting.

### 4. Storyblok

**Best for:** marketing teams who edit on a visual canvas.

Storyblok leads with a visual editor, so editors can see the page as they change it. A strong fit if your team lives in a page builder.

### 5. Hygraph

**Best for:** one content source feeding many front ends.

Hygraph is built around a typed API and handles content that has to reach several places at once.

## How to choose

- Want the admin app itself to disappear? **Inhalt**.
- Want a polished editor and do not mind building it? **Sanity**.
- Want to own the whole stack with no seat bill? **Strapi**.
- Live in a visual page builder? **Storyblok**.
- One content source feeding many front ends? **Hygraph**.

If it is the dashboard you are tired of, Inhalt keeps the typed content and the review step and drops the app. See [getting started](/docs/getting-started) for how it reads in practice.
