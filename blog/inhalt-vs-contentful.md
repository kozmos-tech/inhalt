Contentful and Inhalt both give you typed content and a clean API to read from any front end. The difference is how the content gets edited. Contentful hands your team a hosted web app to click through. Inhalt has no app at all. You change content by chatting with the AI client you already use.

Inhalt is ours, so read this as the honest case and weigh it yourself.

## The short version

- **Contentful** is a hosted, well known content platform with a web app your editors log into.
- **Inhalt** is open source and has no editing app. Your content is exposed through the Model Context Protocol, so an AI client reads it and makes changes by chatting.
- Both keep content in typed fields. Both let any front end read through an API.

## Where they line up

- **Typed content.** Both hold your content in defined fields, not loose text.
- **Read from anywhere.** Both split the content from the site, so you build the front end however you want.
- **A review step.** Both let you stage a change and publish it when it is ready.

## Where they part ways

### Editing

Contentful gives your team a dashboard to fill in. Inhalt drops the dashboard. You say what you want changed, and the AI client makes the edit for you. There is no new tool for anyone to learn.

### Who runs it

Contentful is hosted on their cloud, on their plan. Inhalt is one container you run on your own database and your own keys. Nothing leaves your setup.

### Cost as you grow

Contentful charges by seats and usage, and the bill tends to climb as the team and content grow. Inhalt is open source, so there is no seat bill. You pay for the hosting you already run.

## When Contentful is the better fit

- You want a hosted app and would rather not run anything yourself.
- Your editors like a fixed dashboard and the training that comes with it.
- You need the wide set of built in tools and support a large platform brings.

## When Inhalt is the better fit

- You are tired of paying per seat and want to own your setup.
- You would rather change content by chatting than click through a dashboard.
- You want your content and keys to stay on your own database.

## The bottom line

If a hosted app and a big platform are what you want, Contentful is a safe pick. If you would rather drop the dashboard, keep your data, and manage content from the client you already talk to, that is what Inhalt is for. Start with [getting started](/docs/getting-started).
